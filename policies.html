Government Initiatives & Scholarships



Welcome to a gateway of opportunities for a brighter tomorrow. This page unfolds the tapestry of government policies aimed at uplifting communities through education, healthcare, and overall well-being. Discover the scholarships that can be a stepping stone towards a future filled with possibilities. Let's embark on a journey of empowerment together.

1. National Scheme of Incentive to Girls for Secondary Education (NSIGSE):
  • Aims to promote the enrollment of girl students in secondary education and reduce gender disparities
  • Key points: Cash incentive for completing secondary education, financial support for purchase of books and uniforms
  • Application: Automatic for eligible girl students enrolled in government and government-aided schools


2. National Scholarship Portal (NSP):
  • Provides a single platform for various scholarships offered by the central government, state governments, and other government agencies.
  • Key points: Offers scholarships for students at different education levels based on merit and means.
  • Application: Register and apply online on the NSP website (scholarships.gov.in).


3. Pradhan Mantri Scholarship Scheme for Central Armed Police Forces and Assam Rifles (PMSS):
  • Offers scholarships to the wards of CAPF and Assam Rifles personnel martyred during duty.
  • Key points: Financial support for higher education, including professional courses.
  • Application: Apply through the respective CAPF or Assam Rifles welfare authorities.

Pratham:
Pratham is one of the largest NGOs working towards the provision of quality education to underprivileged children in India. Their initiatives include the "Read India" campaign, "Learning Camps," and "Pratham Institute," which focus on literacy and numeracy skills among children and youth
  • Pratham's programs are often implemented in collaboration with local schools or community centers.
  • Beneficiaries can directly participate in Pratham's learning camps or vocational training programs by contacting their local Pratham chapter or visiting their website for more information.

    • Akshara Foundation:

    Akshara Foundation works to ensure every child has access to quality education, focusing on foundational literacy and numeracy skills. They implement programs such as "School Readiness Program" and "Library in Every School" to improve learning outcomes among underprivileged children

  • Schools and communities can collaborate with Akshara Foundation to implement their programs and establish libraries.
  • Parents and children can participate in Akshara Foundation's educational programs by enrolling in their community-based initiatives or contacting their local offices for support.




    • Dr. Hargobind Khurana Scholarship Scheme:

  • This scholarship is offered to students belonging to economically weaker sections of society for pursuing higher education
  • Key points: Financial assistance for tuition fees, books, and other educational expenses.
  • Application: Apply through the Department of Welfare of Scheduled Castes, Backward Classes & Minorities, Government of Punjab.


    • Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme for Scheduled Caste Students:

  • Provides financial assistance to students belonging to Scheduled Caste (SC) communities for pursuing post-matriculation education.
  • Key points: Covers tuition fees, maintenance allowance, and other incidental expenses.
  • Application: Apply online through the National Scholarship Portal (NSP) or the respective State Government scholarship portal.

    • Mai Bhago Scheme for Girls' Education:

  • Aims to improve girls' education by providing financial assistance to economically backward families for educating their daughters.
  • Key points: Financial support for tuition fees, books, and other educational expenses.
  • Application: Apply through the Department of Education, Government of Punjab.
